Our History

It all started in the year 1778, when our founder, Samuel Adams, discovered his love for the mystical sport, Soccer (or as he called it, Picky and Kicky). He was amazed at the rules of the sport and the conflict and factions created as a result of the sport being played. That, along with his love for lagers (and a somewhat steamy love with loggers), is what defined generations of associated football. During the Civil War era, Samuel Adam's grandchild, Samuel Adams, revolutionized the jersey industry through his use of workers of color and his philosophies that even colored workers should be treated like the whites of just 40 years prior. During the WWI era, Samuel Adams--the grandchild--had a son who took over the family-owned business. He stopped producing jerseys for the war, and instead began to create army uniforms for the powers that needed them. It flipped the Axes of the war. During WWII, Samuel Adams's son, Samuel Adams, was a decorated naval officer who took over the business after the war ended. He noticed that the use of sloths in the jersey business would be a risky venture, but that it could potentially pay off. The company went bankrupt the next year due to unrelated causes. There is a 70 year gap in the history of Premier Jerseys, but the business is now back up and running. Currently owned by Brian "Depresso Expresso" La Torre, Premier Jerseys has opened its 30th location in the Washington D.C. area. With his army of jersey shops, he has ambitions to control the jersey economy, and eventually the rest of the world.
